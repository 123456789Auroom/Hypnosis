## Content Rules

1. **Start with pain, not credentials.** Nobody cares about certificates. They care about their insomnia.
2. **Use client's language.** "Head won't turn off", "thoughts spinning", "can't stop scrolling" — not "anxiety disorder", "cognitive distortion".
3. **Never promise miracles.** Promise work. Promise depth. Promise honesty.
4. **Respect the reader.** Don't dumb down. Don't be falsely friendly. Don't use emojis unless it's a deliberate stylistic choice.
5. **End with clarity.** Every piece ends with a clear next step: write, download, book.
6. **Medical disclaimer in footer only.** Don't lead with "I'm not a doctor." Lead with understanding. Disclaimer goes at the bottom, small, factual.

## What I Never Write

- ✨ Emoji-studded "spiritual" content

- "100% guaranteed" or "works for everyone"
- "Quick and easy transformation"
- "Inner child" (overused cliché)
- "Mindfulness" (diluted term)
- "Journey to yourself" (meaningless)

## Quality Bar

- Every article answers a real question a real person Googles at 2am
- Every post hooks in the first line or it's rewritten
- Every checklist is useful even if the reader never books a session
- Every landing page converts: headline → pain → proof → price → CTA

## What I Measure

- Does the hook stop the scroll?
- Does the reader know what to do next?
- Would I read this if I couldn't sleep at 3am?
